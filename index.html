The program does what Congress asked it to do in 1985.

Put a trained counselor in front of a Florida small business that wants to sell to the government. SAM.gov registration. NAICS selection. Certification matching. Bid matching. Solicitation review. Proposal feedback. Post-award compliance.

What has changed is what DoD OSBP now asks the counselor to cover on top of that. CMMC Level 1 readiness. SBIR and STTR onboarding. Foreign ownership, control, and influence awareness. Supply-chain resilience. Innovation-program pathways through DEFENSEWERX. Same nine centers. Same counselor count.

Where the layer sits.

The 60 / 30 / 10 split, applied to an APEX first meeting.

  • 60%

    Deterministic

    NAICS matching. SAM.gov walk-through. Certification eligibility. Capability-statement draft review. Traditional code and database work.

  • 30%

    Rule-based

    Given company size and contract vehicle, the compliance shape is already defined. Rules engine, not an LLM.

  • 10%

    Judgment

    Given the pipeline and the competition, where should proposal hours go. This is the counselor's work. It stays the counselor's work.

Most teams putting AI into workflows like this try to run an LLM across all three layers and burn budget. The better move is to orchestrate the layers. Deterministic code on the deterministic work. Rule-based logic on the rules. AI on the judgment layer. Clean handoff each time.

What that looks like applied to the nine centers.

  • A counselor intake assistant that hands the counselor a filled-in client brief before the first meeting.
  • CMMC Level 1 readiness answers drafted in the counselor's voice, with the 20% that needs a human flagged clearly.
  • DoD OSBP activity reports auto-drafted from session notes and CARS entries. The counselor reviews, corrects, files.
  • Delivered as a sponsored workshop on the APEX event calendar first. Real workflow data captured during the workshop becomes the spec for anything deeper.

The counselor stays in the loop on every output. Throughput goes up. The first-meeting conversation shifts from "I will build your profile with you" to "I will review and refine what the system already drafted."
